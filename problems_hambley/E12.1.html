$\textbf{Question}:\\$ A certain transistor has $\beta = 50, I_{ES} = 10^{-14} \: \text{A}, v_{CE} = 5 \: \text{V},$ and $i_E = 10 \: \text{mA}$. $\\$ Assume that $V_T = 26 \: \text{mV}$. Find $v_{BE}, v_{BC}, i_B, i_C$, and $\alpha$.

$\textbf{Answer}:\\$ From $i_E = I_{ES} \left[ e^{\frac{v_{BE}}{V_T}} - 1 \right]$ we obtain $$ v_{BE} = V_T \ln \left( \frac{i_E}{I_{ES}} + 1 \right) = 26 \: \text{mV} \cdot \ln \left( \frac{10 \: \text{mA}}{10^{-14} \: \text{A}} + 1 \right) = 0.718 \: \text{V}$$ From this, we now compute $$\begin{align}v_{CE} &= v_{CB} + v_{BE} \Leftrightarrow \\\\ v_{CB} &= v_{CE} - v_{BE} = 5 \: \text{V} - 0.718 \: \text{V} = 4.28 \: \text{V} \\\\ v_{BC} &= -v_{CB} = -4.28 \: \text{V} \end{align}$$ From $\beta$ and $i_E$ we compute $i_B$. $$\begin{align}i_E &= (\beta + 1)i_B \Leftrightarrow \\\\ i_B &= \frac{i_E}{(\beta + 1)} = \frac{10 \: \text{mA}}{51} = 0.196 \: \text{mA} \end{align}$$ The collector current is directly computed $$i_C = i_E - i_B = 10 \: \text{mA} - 0.196 \: \text{mA} = 9.804 \: \text{mA} $$ Lastly, $\alpha$ is calculated from one of several relations $$\alpha = \frac{\beta}{\beta + 1} = \frac{i_C}{i_E} = \frac{50}{51} = 0.980 $$
