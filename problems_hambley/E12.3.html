Exercise 12.3

$\textbf{Question}:\\$ A certain transistor operated with forward bias of the base-emitter junction and reverse bias of the base-collector junction has $i_C = 9.5 \: \text{mA}$ and $i_E = 10 \: \text{mA}$. Find the values of $i_B, \alpha, \beta$.

$\textbf{Answer}:\\$ Clearly, we have $$i_B = i_E - i_C = 0.5 \: \text{mA} $$ From this, we calculate $\alpha$. $$\alpha = \frac{i_C}{i_E} = \frac{9.5 \: \text{mA}}{10 \: \text{mA}} = 0.95$$ Because the transistor is operating in the active region, $\beta = \frac{i_C}{i_B}$ is valid. $$\beta = \frac{9.5 \: \text{mA}}{0.5 \: \text{mA}} = 19$$
