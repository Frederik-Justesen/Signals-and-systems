Problem 2.2-1

$\textbf{Question}$: Show whether the step function $u(t)$ is a power signal or an energy signal. <\

$\textbf{Answer}$: A signal $g(t)$ is an energy signal if $$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}g^2(t) \: \text{d}t <\infty \tag1$$ and a power signal if $$\lim_{T\to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \int_{-\frac{T}{2}}^{\frac{T}{2}}g^2(t) \: \text{d}t <\infty \tag2$$ We first check if $(1)$ is satisfied. $$E_g = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}u^2(t) \: \text{d}t = \int_{0}^{ \infty} \: \text{d}t = \Big[ t \Big]^\infty_0 \nless \infty \quad \text{not energy signal}$$ We then check if $(2)$ is satisifed. $$\begin{align}P_g &= \lim_{T\to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \int_{-\frac{T}{2}}^{\frac{T}{2}}u^2(t) \: \text{d}t \\\\ & = \lim_{T\to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \Big[ t \Big]^{\frac{T}{2}}_{-\frac{T}{2}} = \lim_{T\to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \cdot T = 1\end{align}$$ A step function is a power signal with power equal to $1$.
