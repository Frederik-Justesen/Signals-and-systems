Problem 2.1-9

$\textbf{Question}:\\$ Determine the power for the following signals. $$\begin{align}(\textbf{a}) \quad g_a(t) &= 5 \cos\left(300\pi t + \frac{\pi}{6} \right) \\\\ (\textbf{b}) \quad g_b(t) &= 5 \cos(100\pi t) + 2\sin(200\pi t) \\\\ (\textbf{c}) \quad g_c(t) &= 5 \cos\left(100\pi t + \frac{\pi}{3} \right) + 2\sin(200\pi t) \\\\ (\textbf{d}) \quad g_d(t) &= 5 \sin(55t)\sin(\pi t)\\\\ (\textbf{e}) \quad g_e(t) &= \Big[10 \sin(5t)\cos(10t) \Big]u(t) \\\\ (\textbf{f}) \quad g_f(t) &= e^{j\alpha t}\sin(\omega_0 t) \end{align}$$

$\textbf{Answer}:\\$ To solve these problems, we will use the results from Problem 2.1-7 and Problem 2.1-8. They say that $$\begin{align}\text{power} \bigg\{ C_1 \cos(\omega_1 t + \theta_1) + C_2 \cos(\omega_2 t + \theta_2) \bigg\} &= \frac{C_1^2 + C_2^2}{2} \quad \omega_1 \neq \omega_2 \tag1\\\\ \text{power} \bigg\{ \sum_{k=m}^n D_k e^{j\omega_k t} \bigg\} &= \sum_{k=m}^n |D_k|^2 \tag2 \end{align}$$

$(\textbf{a}) \\$ It follows from $(1)$ that $$P_{g_a} = \frac{5^2}{2} = 12.5$$ $(\textbf{b}) \\$ Similarly $$P_{g_b} = \frac{5^2}{2} + \frac{2^2}{2} = 14.5 $$ $(\textbf{c}) \\$ Phase shifts has no influence on the power, as long as the sinusoids have different frequencies. $$P_{g_c} = \frac{5^2}{2} + \frac{2^2}{2} = 14.5 $$ $(\textbf{d}) \\$ Using that $\frac{1}{2j} \big[e^{j\theta}-e^{-j\theta}\big] = \sin(\theta)$ we obtain $$\begin{align}5\sin(55t)\sin(\pi t) &= 5 \cdot \frac{1}{2j} \big[ e^{j55t} - e^{-j55t} \big] \cdot \frac{1}{2j}\big[ e^{j\pi t} - e^{-j\pi t} \big] \\\\ &= -\frac{5}{4}\Big( e^{j\omega_1 t} + e^{j\omega_2 t} + e^{j\omega_3 t} + e^{j\omega_4 t} \Big)\end{align}$$ The actual values of $\omega_1, \omega_2$ etc. don't matter because the signal is now on the form $$\sum_{k=1}^4 -\frac{5}{4} e^{j\omega_k t} $$ Hence, the power is $$P_{g_d} = \sum_{k=1}^4 \left|-\frac{5}{4} \right|^2 = 6.25$$ $(\textbf{e}) \\$ Similarly, we can show that $$g_e = \sum_{k=1}^{4} -\frac{10}{4} e^{j\omega_k t} u(t) $$ Because the signal is only active for $t\geq 0$, (i.e. half the duration of a pure cosine) the power is $$P_{g_e} = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{k=1}^4 \left| -\frac{10}{4} \right| = 12.5$$ $(\textbf{f}) \\$ $$\begin{align}g_f &= e^{j\alpha t}\sin(\omega_0 t) = e^{j\alpha t} \frac{1}{2j} \big( e^{j\omega_0 t} - e^{-j\omega_0 t} \big) \\\\ &= \sum_{k=1}^2 \frac{1}{2j} e^{j\omega_k t} \end{align}$$ The power is thus $$P_{g_f} = \sum_{k=1}^2 \left| \frac{1}{2j} \right|^2 = \frac{1}{2}$$
