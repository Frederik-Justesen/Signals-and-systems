Problem 2.1-3

$\textbf{Question}:$ Find the average power of the signals in Fig. 2.1-3.

$\textbf{Answer}:$ For $\phi(t)$, we notice that it is a periodic signal and that averaging from $-\infty$ to $\infty$ yields the same as averaging over one period, hence $$\begin{align}P_\phi &= \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi}(e^{-t})^2 \: \text{d}t = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi}e^{-2t} \: \text{d}t \\\\ &= -\frac{1}{2\pi}\Big[ e^{-2t} \Big]_0^\pi = -\frac{1}{2\pi}(e^{-2}-e^0) \\\\ &= \frac{1-e^{-2\pi}}{2\pi} \approx 0.159\end{align}$$ For $w_0(t)$, we recall that the sign of the signal has no influence on the power. Hence, the alternation between $-1$ and $1$ yields the same power as if the signal was constant $1$. Therefore, $$P_{w_o} = \frac{1}{T_0}\int_{0}^{T_0}w_0^2(t) \: \text{d}t = \frac{1}{T_0}\int_{0}^{T_0} \: \text{d}t = \frac{1}{T_0} T_0 = 1$$
