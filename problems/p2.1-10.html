Problem 2.1-10

$\textbf{Question}:\\$ Find the power of the signals in the figure below.

$\textbf{Answer}:\\$ $(\textbf{a}) \\$ The sign of a piecewise constant function has no impact on the power. Hence, for the square wave with period $T=4$ we get $$P_a = \frac{1}{4} \int_{0}^{4} (\pm 1)^2 \: \text{d}t = 1$$ $(\textbf{b}) \\$ $$P_b = \frac{1}{10\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} 1^2 \: \text{d}t = \frac{1}{5}$$ $(\textbf{c}) \\$ Here, the function is $\frac{1}{2\pi}t$ with period $2\pi$. The power is $$\begin{align}P_c &= \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left(\frac{1}{2\pi}t \right)^2 \: \text{d}t \\\\ &= \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \Big[\frac{1}{3}t^3 \Big]_0^{2\pi} \\\\ &= \frac{1}{3} \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} (2\pi)^3 \\\\ &= \frac{1}{3}\end{align}$$ $(\textbf{d}) \\$ When squaring this periodic function (with period $\pi$), it becomes an even function, hence we only need to integrate over half the period. $$\begin{align}P_d &= \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \left(\frac{4}{\pi}t \right)^2 \: \text{d}t \\\\ &= \frac{1}{2}\frac{4 \cdot 4^2}{\pi^3} \Big[ \frac{1}{3}t^3 \Big]_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}} \\\\ &= \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{4}{\pi}\right)^3 \left(\frac{\pi}{4} \right)^3 \cdot \frac{1}{3} \\\\ &= \frac{1}{6} \end{align}$$ $(\textbf{e}) \\$ $$P_e = \frac{1}{3}\int_0^1 t^2 \: \text{d}t = \frac{1}{9}$$ $(\textbf{f}) \\$
