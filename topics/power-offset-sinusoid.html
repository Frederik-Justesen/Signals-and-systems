Power of $g(t) = A\cos(\omega_0 t + \theta) + B$

General formula for power of a real signal $$P_g = \lim_{T\to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \int_{\frac{T}{2}}^{\frac{T}{2}} |g(t)|^2 \: \text{d}t$$ Because $g(t)$ is periodic, averaging $g^2(t)$ over an infinitely large interval is identical to averaging over one period $T_0 = \frac{2\pi}{\omega_0}$. $$P_g = \frac{1}{T_0} \int_{0}^{T_0} A^2\cos^2(\omega_0 t + \theta) + 2AB\cos(\omega_0 t + \theta) + B^2 \: \text{d}t$$ Using the identity $\cos^2 x = \frac{1 + \cos(2x)}{2}$ gives $$\begin{align}P_g &= \frac{1}{T_0} \int_{0}^{T_0} \frac{A^2}{2} (1 + \cos(2\omega_0 t + 2\theta)) + 2AB\cos(\omega_0 t + \theta) + B^2 \: \text{d}t \\\\ &= \frac{1}{T_0} \left( \int_{0}^{T_0} \frac{A^2}{2} \: \text{d}t + \int_{0}^{T_0} \frac{A^2}{2} \cos(2\omega_0 t + 2\theta) \: \text{d}t + \int_{0}^{T_0} 2AB\cos(\omega_0 t + \theta) \: \text{d}t + \int_{0}^{T_0} B^2 \: \text{d}t \right) \\\\ \end{align}$$ From Power of sinusoid we already know that $$\begin{align} \int_{0}^{T_0} \frac{A^2}{2} \cos(2\omega_0 t + 2\theta) \: \text{d}t &= 0 \\\\ \int_{0}^{T_0} 2AB\cos(\omega_0 t + \theta) \: \text{d}t &=0 \end{align} $$ $$\begin{align}P_g &= \frac{1}{T_0} \int_{0}^{T_0} \frac{A^2}{2} \: \text{d}t + \frac{1}{T_0} \int_{0}^{T_0} B^2 \: \text{d}t \\\\ &= \frac{A^2}{2} + B^2\end{align}$$ Thus, the power of a sinusoid with offset is the sum of the power of the sinusoid and the power of the offset: $$\boxed{P_g = \frac{A^2}{2} + B^2}$$ Notice that the phase shift $\theta$ does not affect the power.
